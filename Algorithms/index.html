Algorithms Used

  • Quick Sort
  • Heap Sort
  • Floyd-Warshall
  • Greedy Algorithm for Stock Distribution
  • Randomization Algorithm

Details of Algorithms

Quick Sort

Purpose: To sort manufacturers based on attributes such as stock or demand.

Function:

  • Recursively partitions the array using a pivot.
  • Ensures the array is sorted in ascending or descending order as needed.
  • Facilitates efficient stock allocation.

Heap Sort

Purpose: To organize suppliers into a max heap for stock allocation.

Function:

  • Builds a max heap to ensure the supplier with the largest stock is at the root.
  • Removes and assigns the root iteratively, ensuring prioritized allocation.

Floyd-Warshall

Purpose: To compute shortest paths between all pairs of distributors and wholesalers.

Function:

  • Utilizes a dynamic programming approach to calculate the shortest distance between nodes.
  • Produces a distance matrix for optimal routing of goods.

Greedy Algorithm for Stock Distribution

Purpose: To distribute stock from wholesalers to retailers, prioritizing high-demand retailers.

Function:

  • Sorts retailers by descending demand.
  • Allocates stock based on available inventory and retailer demand.
  • Stops once stocks are depleted or all demands are met.

Randomization Algorithm

Purpose: To simulate real-world variability by assigning random demand to retailers.

Function:

  • Generates random demand values within a specified range for retailers.
  • Adds unpredictability to the demand scenario.
